Bapsi Sidhwa

Pakistani-American Novelist

Biography of Bapsi Sidhwa Bapsi Sidhwa is a Pakistani-American novelist and women's rights activist. She was born in 1938 in Karachi.

Sidhwa is most famous for the novel Cracking India (1988), but she is the author of several other novels including The Crow Eaters, The Bridge (1982), and Water: A Novel (2006).

Her books often focus on life in colonial India, the history of Partition, experiences of the Parsi community (a small community of Indian Zoroastrians), and the experience of immigrating from South Asia to the United States.
